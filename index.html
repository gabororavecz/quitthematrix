Day trading

Day trading is a short-term trading strategy where traders buy and sell financial instruments, such as stocks, forex, or cryptocurrencies, within the same trading day. The goal is to profit from small price fluctuations, and all positions are closed by the end of the trading day to avoid overnight risks.

Swing trading

Swing trading is a trading strategy that involves holding financial instruments like stocks, currencies, or commodities for several days to weeks to capture medium-term price movements. Unlike day trading, where traders close all positions by the end of the day, swing traders aim to profit from price swings over longer time frames. Here's an in-depth look at swing trading

Position trading

Position trading is a long-term trading strategy in which traders hold assets for months to years to capture significant price trends. Unlike short-term strategies like day trading or scalping, position trading is less about short-term fluctuations and more about riding large price movements over extended periods. This strategy is popular among investors who want to minimize the time spent analyzing markets daily while benefiting from longer-term market trends.
